---
title: China's One Country, Two Systems: Hong Kong, Macau and Taiwan
slug: one-country-two-systems
---

One country, two systems links constitutional design, sovereignty, treaty transition, autonomy, national security, and reunification policy. Hong Kong and Macau provide the institutional practice; Taiwan remains the unresolved and more contested application.

This guide separates the three settings while keeping them in one analytical frame: basic laws and SAR practice, PRC white papers on Taiwan, and the changing legal meaning of autonomy under Chinese sovereignty.
